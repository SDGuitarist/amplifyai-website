Human-Led AI curriculum

Clearer thinking before better prompting.

People usually blame the prompt when AI misses the point. The problem often started earlier. The goal was vague, the standard stayed in someone’s head, or the system had to invent the missing context.

Human-Led AI teaches people how to make that thinking visible, communicate it clearly, and remain responsible for what comes back.

Talk about a pilot or workshop

The constitution

The goal is not to teach people how to use AI. It is to teach them how to communicate human expertise, judgment, and intent so intelligent systems amplify human capability without replacing human thinking.

Phase one

Learning to communicate

The teaching order starts with a practical result, then follows the questions people naturally ask once they see the difference.

  1. 01

    How do I get better AI output?

    Prompt Engineering

    Use the Five-Layer Framework to replace vague requests with Identity, World, Task, Example, and Constraint.

    Better instructions
  2. 02

    Why do better prompts work?

    Intent Engineering

    Clarify the problem, audience, standard, assumptions, and boundaries before asking a system to act.

    Clearer thinking
  3. 03

    What is the model doing?

    Thinking Like the Model

    Build an intuitive picture of prediction, ambiguity, examples, and why fluent output can still be wrong.

    Better mental models
  4. 04

    How do professionals work with it?

    Expert AI Collaboration

    Direct research, drafting, and critique inside a workflow with verification and human rulings built in.

    Professional judgment
