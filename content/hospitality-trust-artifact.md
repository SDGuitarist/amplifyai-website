# How It Works: Building a Guest Experience Calendar with AI

## The situation

A 3-property boutique hotel group in a coastal city. Each property has its own vibe, its own neighborhood, its own guests. The GM wants to run cultural programming -- local artist pop-ups, wellness mornings, live music nights, seasonal food events -- but there's no creative director on staff. The events coordinator left six months ago. Since then, programming has been "whatever the front desk manager can pull together the week before."

The result: generic wine-and-cheese nights that don't match the property's identity. Guests post about the room, not the experience. The properties blend into the OTA listings.

## What we'd build together

**Week 1: Experience Audit.** I walk each property and map what makes it distinct. Neighborhood, guest demographics, seasonal patterns, existing vendor relationships. This is the same read-the-room process I've used for 30 years producing live events. You can't program a space you haven't felt.

**Weeks 2-3: AI-Powered Programming Engine.** We build a system that:
- Pulls from a curated database of local artists, chefs, wellness practitioners, and cultural partners
- Matches programming to each property's identity and guest profile
- Generates a 90-day experience calendar with event concepts, vendor suggestions, and estimated costs
- Adapts the calendar based on guest feedback, seasonal shifts, and booking patterns

The AI does the research and assembly. The human judgment -- what feels right for this room, this crowd, this neighborhood -- comes from experience. That's the part you can't automate. That's the part I bring.

**Weeks 4+: Staff Playbooks.** Each event gets a one-page playbook: setup, flow, guest touchpoints, fallback plan if the vendor cancels. Your front desk team can execute without needing an events background.

## What changes

The hotel goes from "we should do something" to a running calendar that reflects each property's character. Programming becomes a system, not a scramble. Guests start posting about the experience, not just the room.

No chatbots. No revenue management dashboards. Just a creative system that runs because the hard part -- knowing what makes a good live experience -- is built into the foundation.
