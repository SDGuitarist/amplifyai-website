The method

Human at the start.
AI in the middle.
Human at the end.

The useful question is not, “Can AI do this?” It is, “What does good look like, what can go wrong, and who is responsible for the result?”

  1. Start
    Name the standard.

    Your intent, constraints, taste, rights, and definition of good come first.

  2. Middle
    Let the tool do bounded work.

    Research, options, organization, drafts, and pattern-finding inside rules you understand.

  3. End
    Keep judgment in the room.

    Check the work, reject what misses, document decisions, and put your name only on what you can defend.

About Alex

A working creative who built the systems first.

I am a Berklee-trained composer and working musician with more than 30 years in live performance. I started building AI systems because the business around the creative work kept eating the time meant for the work itself.

What began with lead responses, research, writing, and business operations became a larger question: how do you use these tools without giving away the judgment that made your work valuable in the first place?

That is what I teach now. Not a tour of whichever tool launched this week. A way to think, decide, and build that still works when the tools change.
